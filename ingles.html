DIGITAL ART TECHNIQUES

Digital art has revolutionized the way artists create and express their ideas. Through different techniques, creators can develop impressive visual works using modern technology. Below, we explore some of the most popular techniques in contemporary digital art.

Vector Art
Uses graphics based on lines and geometric shapes, allowing images to be scaled without losing quality. It's ideal for logos and graphic design. The most popular programs for creating vector art include Adobe Illustrator, Inkscape, and CorelDRAW.
Pixel Art
Based on the precise placement of pixels to create images with a retro style, widely used in video games and animations. This style emerged in the early days of video games due to technical limitations, but today it is appreciated as a distinctive art form with nostalgic charm.
Generative Art
Uses algorithms and programming to generate unique images, combining human creativity and technology. Artists create systems of rules and algorithms that determine aspects of the work, but the final result often contains unpredictable elements, creating unique works of art each time the code is executed.
3D Modeling
Allows the creation of three-dimensional representations used in animation, video games, and industrial design. 3D artists use specialized software to model objects using polygons, creating complete virtual worlds with textures, lighting, and effects that simulate reality or create fantastic visions.
Digital Airbrushing
Used to retouch and perfect images, correcting imperfections and enhancing colors in photography and advertising. Artists use tools such as digital brushes that simulate traditional airbrushes, allowing precise control over color application and creating smooth transitions between tones.

Resources for Artists
